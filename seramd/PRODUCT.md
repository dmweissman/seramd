# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Prospective Seramd patients, early private-beta leads, and medically curious visitors evaluating whether a bloodwork-powered peptide telehealth platform feels credible enough to join. They are likely comparing this against wellness clinics, telehealth brands, compounding-pharmacy services, and high-hype supplement marketing.

## Product Purpose

Seramd is a public coming-soon site for a peptide telehealth platform built around labs, licensed clinician review, patient-specific pharmacy fulfillment, and follow-up care. The homepage should collect qualified waitlist interest while making the service feel premium, clinical, compliant, and careful. Success means a visitor quickly understands that Seramd is not a miracle-cure pitch or AI-prescribing shortcut, and feels comfortable leaving an email for private beta updates.

## Brand Personality

Clinical, premium, restrained. The voice should feel medically literate and high-trust without becoming cold, academic, or overpromising. Visuals can feel cinematic and biotech-forward, but the copy should stay grounded in what the service actually does.

## Anti-references

Do not make the brand feel scammy, hype-driven, or full of unsupported claims. Avoid crypto/AI hype aesthetics, supplement-bro performance marketing, generic SaaS landing-page filler, miracle-cure medical advertising, aggressive scarcity tactics, and vague wellness promises. Avoid language that implies guaranteed outcomes, AI-prescribed medication, or treatment without licensed clinician judgment.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with trust before excitement. The site can look advanced, but it should first communicate medical care, clinician oversight, and compliance.
2. Show the clinical system, not fantasy outcomes. Use labs, protocols, follow-up, fulfillment, and review as the substance of the page.
3. Keep claims conservative and specific. Prefer concrete workflow language over hype, superlatives, and broad health promises.
4. Make the private beta feel selective, not manipulative. The waitlist should feel calm and legitimate, with clear expectations and no pressure tactics.
5. Preserve the dark clinical biotech identity. Molecular imagery, graphite surfaces, aqua light, and vial/dashboard assets are part of the current brand signal.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA as the working baseline. Maintain strong contrast on dark surfaces, visible focus states, keyboard-friendly navigation and forms, meaningful alt text for imagery, non-color-only status cues, and reduced-motion support for animated molecular or interface effects. Avoid visual effects that make medical copy harder to read.
